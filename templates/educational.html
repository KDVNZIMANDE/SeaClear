Introduction to Water Quality

Water quality refers to the condition of water, including its chemical, physical, and biological characteristics, usually with respect to its suitability for a particular purpose. Ensuring good water quality is essential for health, environmental sustainability, and economic development.

Why It's Important

Maintaining high water quality is crucial because it directly impacts human health, ecosystems, and economies. Poor water quality can lead to waterborne diseases, harm aquatic life, and negatively affect tourism and local economies. Proper management and monitoring help mitigate these risks and promote a healthy environment.
